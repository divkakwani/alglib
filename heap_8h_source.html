16 /*
17  * Abstract class for a heap
18  * Any heap must conform to this interface
19  *
20  * There are two types of ordering of the type elt_t - natural ordering and
21  * priority ordering. The operator< defines the natural ordering and the
22  * keys associated with the elements define the priority ordering. The former
23  * ordering is defined by the class and the latter by the client of this
24  * library.
25  * The natural ordering is used to establish the equality of two elements. If
26  * none of the two elements is greater than the other, then they are equal.
27  * Hence, the `min_heap` class is configurable with different schemes of
28  * priority ordering by supplying corresponding keys. In case, none is
29  * supplied, the ordering chosen by the `min_heap` defaults to natural
30  * ordering.
31  *
32  * The reason of having another variable `key` for ordering the
33  * elements is to decouple the scheme of ordering from the
34  * object itself.
35  * If we use operator< of elt's class, it would be far too
36  * restrictive. It would allow only one type of ordering - the
37  * one defined by the class. In many situations, that may not be
38  * the scheme we require. Worse still, it precludes any non-comparable
39  * item from being used to make a min-heap.
40  * To make this more concrete, consider a use-case in which a shopkeeper
41  * services the requests of his customers. The customers are of Person type
42  * that doesn't have any total-ordering relation. The shopkeeper, however,
43  * can have different schemes of prioritizing his customers, some of them
44  * are -
45  * Prioritize the shop's investor.
46  * Prioritize senior citizens.
47  * Prioritize acquaintances
48  * ... and so on.
49  * The essence of this methodology is that the criterion of ordering should
50  * be specified by the min_heap user, not by the class.
51  *
52  * Why not use a functor?
53  * Using a functor can potentially lead to inconsistency of the heap. Since by
54  * allowing the user to supply a user-defined functor, we allow him to change
55  * the priority order arbitrarily and as many times as the user wishes, but
56  * the heap requires that every change be followed by a sift up or a sift down.
57  *
58  *
59  */
60
